About Me

After 8 years building enterprise products across payments, healthcare, and fintech, I founded Aero Intelligence to solve a problem I kept seeing: AI designed for people at desks, not people in the field. HVAC technicians on ladders. Construction workers in hard hats. People who need information now but can't stop to type.

I build backward from user constraints, not forward from technology. That discipline led me to voice-first interfaces for field workers, a published governance framework for enterprise AI compliance, and a prioritization methodology now used to evaluate 47+ use cases across Legal, Risk, Compliance, and Security. I don't ship features—I remove friction.

My path from data analyst to product manager to technical PM to founder taught me that the best AI products aren't the most sophisticated. They're the ones that understand their users deeply enough to know when high accuracy is good enough—and when it's dangerous. That judgment only comes from years of cross-functional partnership with engineering, data science, and go-to-market teams.

Product Philosophy

How I Build

Start with the user's blocker, not the technology.

I don't ask "what can AI do?"—I ask "what's stopping this user right now?" A technician on a rooftop can't type, so I build voice-first. An executive won't fund what they can't measure, so I lead with ROI. The best products dissolve constraints users didn't know they had.

Prioritize ruthlessly, then earn the buy-in.

When I inherited dozens of AI use cases, I didn't reach for RICE or MoSCoW. I built a weighted prioritization methodology—Internal Demand, Rinse & Repeat potential, Customer Impact, Strategic Fit, Feasibility—then translated those bets into ROI models executives could fund. Strategy isn't what you build. It's what you don't build, and the cross-functional alignment to defend that call.

Build platforms, not projects.

Every feature I ship, I ask: can this serve the next use case too? I architect for reusability—shared services for ingestion, orchestration, evaluation, and guardrails—so each new deployment ships faster and cheaper than the last. Projects solve problems once. Platforms solve them forever.

Measure what matters when AI fails.

Accuracy is table stakes. I build evaluation frameworks that weight consequences—because a hallucination in legal costs more than a mistranslation in a menu. Existing tools like DeepEval and RAGAS measure correctness. I measure business impact of failure, and design human-in-the-loop workflows that catch errors before users do.
