After 8+ years in enterprise tech, I founded Aero Intelligence to deliver Vision and Voice AI to field workers—because technicians on rooftops and in mechanical rooms can't stop to type.

I build products backward from user pain—not forward from technology. Field workers can't type, so I build voice-first. Executives need proof before funding, so I lead with ROI. Product Philosophy

How I Build

Start with the user's blocker, not the technology.

I don't ask 'what can AI do?'—I ask 'what's stopping this user right now?' A technician on a rooftop can't type, so I build voice-first. An executive won't fund what they can't measure, so I lead with ROI.

Prioritize ruthlessly, then earn the buy-in.

Faced with 400+ AI use cases, I built a consequence-weighted framework to surface the 14 that actually mattered. Then I built the business case and secured $7M+ from vertical leaders. Good prioritization isn't just saying no to good ideas—it's translating the best ones into language that gets funded.

Build platforms, not projects.

I discovered that 69% of use cases could ship through 8 reusable platforms—cutting delivery costs by 43%. Every feature I build, I ask: can this serve the next use case too? One investment, many deployments.

Evaluate like failures have consequences.

92.9% accuracy sounds good until you realize 7.1% of users hit failures. I pioneered a consequence-weighted evaluation framework that measures AI by business impact, not just metrics—because a wrong answer in legal costs more than a wrong menu translation.
